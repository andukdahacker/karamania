Join & Name Entry

  • Input: Name only (no avatar, no email)
  • Audio unlock: JOIN tap creates AudioContext
  • Participant dots: Show who's already in
Time budget: <8 seconds from scan to joined

Icebreaker Tap

Phase 2 — Party Card Deal (pre-song moment)
PARTY CARD DEAL

Challenge Card — Singer's View

DJ auto-deals one card per singer. 19 curated challenges across three types: vocal modifiers (blue), performance modifiers (purple), and group involvement (gold). Accept the dare, dismiss it, or burn your one free redraw.

  • Auto-deal: DJ picks from pool, no repeats until exhausted
  • Singer choice: Accept, Dismiss, or Redraw (1 free)
  • Audience sees: "🃏 CHALLENGE INCOMING..." with anticipation
  • Soft deadline: 8s auto-dismiss, never blocks the music
  • Group cards: Pick random participants, announced on all phones
Core differentiator: Every performance becomes unique. "Will they do the dare?" creates anticipation before the first note.

Phase 3 — Song (60-70% of session time)
SONG

Song State — Lean-In Mode

Where users spend most of their time. Phone is a participation device: soundboard taps audible to the room, emoji reactions visible on all screens. Near-silent app — real karaoke is the soundtrack.

  • Soundboard: 6 sounds (3×2 grid), taps audible via Web Audio
  • Reactions: 5 emoji, visible on all screens as scatter
  • Participants: Compact list, activity status
  • Song Over: Host-only, 500ms long-press
  • Audio state: Web Audio graph near-dormant
Attention mode: Lean-in. Casual glances between songs. Phone at side or on table.

SONG → LIGHTSTICK

Lightstick Mode

Phone becomes a concert lightstick. Full-screen animated glow — hold it up and sway. Creates a physical-room concert atmosphere in a dark KTV room when multiple phones glow together. Free-form: no synchronization, each person picks their color.

  • Toggle: One tap from lean-in mode to enter
  • Color picker: 5 dots along bottom, tap to change
  • Hype signal: Still available — flash/strobe button
  • Exit: Tap anywhere or swipe down
  • No sync: Each phone independent — organic feel
Participation: Lightstick mode active time = passive tier (1 pt). The phone is a prop, not a screen.

OVERLAY → CAPTURE

Prompted Media Capture

Floating bubble appears at key moments: reaction peaks, post-ceremony, session start/end. Any participant pops it to capture a photo, video (5s max), or audio snippet. Non-intrusive — ignore it and it fades. Building the raw content pipeline for v3 highlight reels.

  • Trigger: Server-side reaction peak detection
  • Pop → Capture: 2 taps total (pop + select type)
  • Photo: Inline on all browsers
  • Video/Audio: Inline on Chrome, native picker on iOS
  • Upload: Background queue, never blocks party
  • Also: Persistent capture icon in toolbar (manual anytime)
Design rule: Max 1 bubble per 60s. Never during voting or silence phase. Auto-dismiss after 15s.

Phase 4 — Ceremony (the defining experience)
↓ Host long-presses "Song Over!" ↓
CEREMONY → VOTE

Voting Phase (T+0 to T+4s)

4-second hard window. Visual countdown creates game-show urgency. Everyone votes for anyone except themselves. Drumroll builds across all devices.

  • Duration: Exactly 4 seconds, server-enforced
  • Input: Single tap on one nominee
  • Timer: Circular countdown (conic-gradient)
  • Audio: Drumroll building on all devices
  • Sync: Server sends revealAt timestamp at T+0
Attention mode: Active. All eyes on phones.

CEREMONY → SILENCE

The Silence (T+4s to T+6s)

ALL audio cuts. ALL screens go near-black. Just the category name pulsing faintly. The room physically quiets. This is the highest-tension moment — humans mirror device behavior.

  • Duration: 1.5-2 seconds
  • Audio: Complete silence on all devices
  • Visual: Near-black, category pulsing faintly
  • Sync: ±200ms tolerance (imperceptible)
  • Effect: Room goes quiet. Tension peaks.
The magic: When every phone goes dark and silent simultaneously, conversation stops involuntarily.

CEREMONY → REVEAL

The Reveal (T+6s)

Every phone erupts simultaneously. Host phone: full-volume fanfare. Participant phones: 60% crowd roar. Winner's phone: unique sound + extra haptic. The room physically reacts — laughter, pointing, friendly arguing.

  • Animation: scale + elasticOut (800ms)
  • Audio: Spatial — host loud, participants crowd
  • Vote chart: Shows margin of victory
  • Confetti: Sprite sheet scatter (vibe-specific emoji)
  • Winner phone: Unique celebration + share card
Physical reaction target: Laughter + pointing within 3 seconds. "The Friendly Accusation Roar."

CEREMONY → QUICK

Quick Ceremony (~6s)

No voting. DJ auto-selects winner from participation data. Brief anticipation → quick reveal with single chime. The shoutout, not the game show. Always follows a Full ceremony, never leads.

  • No voting: DJ picks from reaction/soundboard data
  • Duration: ~6 seconds total
  • Audio: Single chime, no fanfare
  • Visual: Simpler — no bar chart, no confetti
  • Purpose: Keeps energy up without ceremony fatigue
Max back-to-back: 1 Full + 1 Quick, then mandatory interlude.

Phase 5 — Interlude Games
INTERLUDE → KINGS CUP

Kings Cup — Group Rule Card

A rule card applies to a group based on shared experience. Everyone self-selects — no individual targeting. Perfect for the first 30 minutes: low pressure, high inclusion, Trang-friendly.

  • Mechanic: Group-based, self-selecting
  • Duration: 10 seconds display, then auto-advance
  • No enforcement: If it applies to you, you decide
  • Examples: "Everyone who's been to Đà Lạt..." / "If your song is queued..."
Front-loaded: Played in first 30 min. Universal, no wrong answer, no individual spotlight.

INTERLUDE → DARE PULL

Dare Pull — Random Target

A dare assigned to a random participant. Name reveals with a slot-machine animation for drama. Social dynamics handle opt-outs — if they don't want to, they just laugh it off. Never front-loaded: individual dares only after 30+ minutes when trust is earned.

  • Mechanic: Random participant, random dare
  • Reveal: Slot-machine name cycling → dramatic pause → name
  • Duration: 15 seconds for dare to happen (IRL)
  • No enforcement: App doesn't track completion
Trust gate: Only after minute 30. Individual spotlight requires earned group comfort.

INTERLUDE → QUICK VOTE

Quick Vote — Binary Opinion Poll

A binary opinion question. Everyone votes within 6 seconds. Results reveal as a bar chart. No winner, no loser — just the room discovering where they stand. The most universally engaging interlude: everyone has opinions.

  • Mechanic: Binary choice, 6s hard window
  • Results: Bar chart with vote split + count
  • Duration: 6s voting + 5s results reveal
  • No judgment: Just opinions, no right answer
Trang moment: "Pineapple on pizza?" → strong opinion → taps NEVER → "See?!" First words about the app.

INTERLUDE

Vote for Next Singer

Bridge between ceremony and next song. "Vote for next singer" instead of "vote for next song" — avoids face-saving issues when someone's beloved song loses. Winner taps a genre tag (one tap) before walking to the mic, feeding contextual ceremonies and challenges.

  • Who not what: Vote for the person, not the song
  • Genre tag: Winner taps one genre before singing
  • Contextual DJ: Genre history feeds ceremony categories + challenges
  • Timer: Auto-advances after 15-20 seconds
  • Fallback: If no votes, DJ picks the least-recently-singing person
Cultural design: Vietnamese KTV — voting for a person is encouragement; voting against a song is personal.

Phase 6 — Finale (end of night)
FINALE

End-of-Night Recap

Full 60-second crescendo. Setlist poster assembles song by song. Night stats roll in. Share-ready 9:16 card generates. The morning-after FOMO machine.

  • Stats recap: Performances, ceremonies, reactions, genre mix
  • Singer highlights: Who sang most, genre champions
  • Share card: 9:16 Instagram Story dimensions
  • Audio: Orchestral flourish, full volume all devices
  • Post-MVP: Song-level setlist via music service integration
Morning-after priority: FOMO first (share card), nostalgia second (setlist), pride third (personal stats).
