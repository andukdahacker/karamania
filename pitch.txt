KARAMANIA
The Party Layer for Karaoke

--

THE PROBLEM

Every karaoke night has the same failure modes. The host becomes an unpaid MC — managing the queue, filling dead air, never actually having fun. Non-singers check out by song three, scrolling their phones in the corner. The performer finishes a song to awkward silence. And the best moments of the night? Gone. No one captured them.

Karaoke rooms are social. Karaoke apps are not.

--

THE INSIGHT

Nobody needs another karaoke app. The karaoke machine works fine. What's broken is everything around it — the dead air between songs, the lack of audience participation, the host who can't stop working, and the shy friend who never finds a way in.

The gap isn't the music. It's the party.

--

THE PRODUCT

Karamania is a second-screen companion app that turns karaoke nights into interactive group experiences. It doesn't play music. It doesn't replace anything. It runs alongside whatever karaoke system is already in the room.

How it works:
- Host opens the app and taps "Start Party"
- A QR code appears — everyone scans it
- No downloads, no accounts, no setup — just a browser tab
- The app takes over from there

An invisible DJ engine orchestrates the entire night automatically. Between songs: crowd voting, funny awards, mini-games, dares, and group moments. During songs: live emoji reactions, soundboard effects, and real-time hype from every phone in the room.

The host's only job? Tap "Song Over" when someone finishes singing. One button. That's it. The rest of the night runs itself.

--

WHY IT WORKS

For the host: "The app ran my party for me." She had fun at her own party for the first time.

For the non-singer: 200+ reactions, 30+ soundboard hits, every ceremony voted in. More participation points than two of the singers. Never sang a note. Best time in the room.

For the shy friend: Started by tapping one emoji. An hour later, she's singing a chorus with four others. Posted her award to Instagram: "apparently I'm a karaoke person now???"

For the performer: Song ends. 15-second ceremony. Crowd votes. Award reveals: "Vocal Assassin." A moment card generates — styled like a concert poster. It's in the group chat before he sits down. A friend from another group DMs: "What app is that?"

--

THE MARKET

The global karaoke app market is $7.5B, growing at 10% CAGR. Every player in the space is trying to BE the karaoke system — streaming music, managing lyrics, selling song packs.

Nobody is building the party layer.

Karamania occupies genuine white space: a companion app that makes the social experience better without touching music licensing, hardware, or venue infrastructure. It works at any karaoke venue, with any system, anywhere.

--

THE FLYWHEEL

The product's shareable output IS the acquisition channel. Every setlist poster shared to Instagram Stories, every moment card sent to a group chat — that's organic marketing baked into features people already want to use.

A friend sees a "Vocal Assassin" moment card. Asks "what app is that?" The answer is a QR code at the next karaoke night. Zero friction from discovery to first use.

--

GO-TO-MARKET

Vietnam first. HCMC and Hanoi have massive karaoke cultures — friend groups hit karaoke rooms weekly. The target: 500+ parties per month in these two cities within 12 months.

Launch strategy: One real friend group. One karaoke night. If they say "we're doing this again" — we have product-market fit. Then their friends host their own nights. Then their friends' friends.

Viral coefficient target: 0.3 at 3 months, 0.5 at 12 months.

--

THE BET

One technical innovation: the server-authoritative DJ engine — a real-time state machine that orchestrates a multi-hour social experience with automatic gap-filling, activity cycling, and dead air prevention. No existing product does this for in-person social events.

Everything else is a design decision executed well or poorly. One bet, not four.
